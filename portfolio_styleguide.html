Color Palette

Blue Color Palette

Blue color schemes convey tranquility, trust, and professionalism. Lighter tints, such as sky blue, signify tranquility, but darker tones, such as navy, imply stability and power. It also represents dependability and security, which is why it is often used in corporate logos and technologies. In contrast, darker or desaturated blues can evoke feelings of sadness or melancholy, particularly in art and literature. Blue is also connected with cooling and refreshment, and is frequently used in the branding of water-related items.

#1 project: MODU company website

This project was created through the course IAT 339 Web design and Development. The project undertaken for the course IAT 339 Web Design and Development involved the creation of a website for MODU, a company specializing in modular furniture.

The aim was to design a fully responsive website with real content, optimized for viewing on all devices. This included structured pages for about, contact, product listing, product detailed pages, blog, and an entire process for purchasing a product, along with appropriate means for users to provide feedback.

The team consisted of myself and one other teammate, Jalene Pang. My role encompassed various aspects, including coding, design (UI), content (UX), and user testing. The process began with analyzing the project's requirements and identifying potential problems.

One of the challenges encountered was with the blog page, which needed to engage users and set the website apart from others in the industry. Through research and user testing, we discovered that integrating a blog into the website could enhance user engagement and community involvement, thereby increasing the likelihood of product purchases.

Another challenge was encountered with the product detail page, which initially had a confusing layout. After receiving feedback from user testing, we made revisions to improve usability and clarity, ultimately creating a layout that effectively showcased the product details.

The significance of this project lies in its ability to create a user-friendly website that aligns with user expectations and enhances the overall user experience. By addressing challenges and continuously iterating on the design, we were able to deliver a high-quality website that reflects MODU's brand identity effectively.

#2 project: Eco Guardian Application User interface

The project undertaken for the course IAT 334 Interface Design involved the creation of an application interface for Eco Guardians, an app targeted at helping people prevent wildfires. The context of the project was to design a user-friendly interface for a scanning/camera-based app that allows users to assess their surroundings for potential fire risks.

The team consisted of myself and two other teammates, Dana Alhabbash and Tiffany Jiazhen Wu. My role encompassed UI design, interaction design, prototyping, and research.

One challenge we encountered was specifying the need for the application. Initially, there was limited risk associated with campfires evolving into forest fires, prompting us to research and adjust the scope of the app. We refined the app to categorize fire levels through scanning,enabling users to identify various fire risks.

Another challenge was with the camera function, which initially seemed limiting. Through research and analysis of other applications, we adapted the scanning feature to complement the camera function, giving users the option to choose both.

The significance of this project lies in providing campers and hikers with an offline/online resource for quick personal analysis of fire risks. By enhancing user flow and providing easy access to fire risk assessments, users can now identify the level of risk in their surroundings and take appropriate precautions.
