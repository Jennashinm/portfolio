Typography

JetBrains Mono

JetBrains Mono is a monospaced typeface created exclusively for coders. JetBrains, the firm behind prominent IDEs such as IntelliJ IDEA, PyCharm, and PhpStorm, built it. The font is intended to improve the coding experience by ensuring excellent readability, particularly during long coding sessions.

Color Palette

Blue Color Palette

Blue color schemes convey tranquility, trust, and professionalism. Lighter tints, such as sky blue, signify tranquility, but darker tones, such as navy, imply stability and power. It also represents dependability and security, which is why it is often used in corporate logos and technologies. In contrast, darker or desaturated blues can evoke feelings of sadness or melancholy, particularly in art and literature. Blue is also connected with cooling and refreshment, and is frequently used in the branding of water-related items.

Through this project, I gained insights into the importance of user-centric design and the role of technology in addressing real-world challenges. By focusing on enhancing user flow and accessibility to fire risk assessments, I learned how to prioritize user needs and create intuitive interfaces. Additionally, I developed skills in conducting domain research, adapting design solutions based on feedback, and collaborating effectively within a team setting. #2 project: Eco Guardian Application User interface

Eco Guardians is an app aimed to help people prevent wildfires by providing a user-friendly interface for assessing potential fire risks in their surroundings using scanning or camera-based technology. The team consisted of myself and two other teammates, Dana Alhabbash and Tiffany Jiazhen Wu. My role encompassed UI design, interaction design, prototyping, and research. We began by analyzing the project requirements and identifying potential challenges. This included conducting domain user research to understand the app's purpose and features. After a few weeks of designing in figma and presenting our ideas, we then developed an interaction framework and designed the UI to facilitate user interaction effectively through Protopie application. An interactive prototype was created and refined based on user pilot testing and feedback. One challenge faced was defining the app's necessity. Initially, there was limited perceived risk associated with campfires escalating into forest fires, prompting us to conduct additional research and adjust the app's scope. We refined the app to categorize fire levels through scanning, enabling users to identify various fire risks accurately. Another challenge was within the camera function, which initially seemed restrictive. Through research and analysis of similar applications, we adapted the scanning feature to complement the camera function, offering users the flexibility to choose between both options. Through this project, I gained insights into the importance of user-centric design and the role of technology in addressing real-world challenges. By focusing on enhancing user flow and accessibility to fire risk assessments, I learned how to prioritize user needs and create intuitive interfaces. Additionally, I developed skills in conducting domain research, adapting design solutions based on feedback, and collaborating effectively within a team setting. This project has provided me with valuable experience in designing applications that not only meet user needs but also contribute to enhancing safety and awareness in outdoor environments
